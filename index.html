Revolutionizing textiles through nanotechnology

Founded in 2013 following over a decade of research at Bar-Ilan University, Sonovia (formerly Nano-Textile Ltd.) developed a patented ultrasonic fabric-finishing technology that permanently embeds zinc oxide nanoparticles into textiles — creating fabrics with powerful antiviral, antibacterial, and antifungal properties with zero chemical binders.

During the COVID-19 pandemic, Sonovia's SonoMask became globally recognized. Lab tests at the internationally accredited ATCCR laboratory confirmed the SonoMask eliminated 99.34% of SARS-CoV-2 within 30 minutes of exposure. The company sold over one million masks to more than 300,000 clients worldwide and donated tens of thousands to first-responders and hospitals. Sonovia listed on the Tel Aviv Stock Exchange in December 2020.

Today, Sonovia is applying its sono-finishing platform to sustainable denim processing — reducing water consumption by up to 95% compared with conventional wet-finishing, and eliminating toxic chemical waste at industrial scale.

Liberating patients from insulin dependency

Founded in 2015 and headquartered at the Jerusalem Bio-Park, Betalin Therapeutics is developing the Engineered Micro Pancreas (EMP) — designed to free the more than 500 million people living with diabetes from daily insulin dependency. The technology was originally developed by Prof. Eduardo Mitrani at the Hebrew University of Jerusalem.

Betalin's EMP combines stem cell technology with tissue engineering, using a decellularized pig lung scaffold to house approximately 200,000 insulin-producing beta cells per implant. The micro-pancreas responds to blood glucose levels by releasing precisely regulated insulin — mimicking the body's natural pancreatic function. Each implant is roughly half a centimeter in diameter and 300 microns thick, inserted subcutaneously under local anesthesia as an outpatient procedure.

Successful preclinical results have been achieved in animal models. The company has raised funding from private investors, the Israel Innovation Authority, and the EU Horizon 2020 programme, and has been named the most innovative biopharma company of the year at the Biomed conference. Two Nobel laureates in chemistry, both living with diabetes, serve on its advisory committee.
